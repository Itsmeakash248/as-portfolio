1. Sampling Principles

Sampling reduces cost and time while preserving representativeness. The objective is to infer population behavior from manageable observations.

  • Population: complete set of units.
  • Sample: selected subset for analysis.
  • Sampling error: natural variation from using a subset.

2. SRSWR and SRSWOR

SRSWR allows repeated selection and keeps draws independent. SRSWOR prevents repetition and creates dependence between draws.

SRSWR count (ordered) = N^n
SRSWOR count (unordered) = NCn

3. Stratified and Cluster Sampling

  • Stratified: divide by characteristic and sample each stratum.
  • Cluster: divide into natural groups and sample clusters.
  • Useful for improving precision or reducing field cost.

Hypothesis Testing Toolkit

Decision making framework under uncertainty.

4. Core Terminology

  • H0: null hypothesis (baseline claim).
  • H1: alternative hypothesis (competing claim).
  • Alpha: significance level (risk of Type I error).
  • Beta: probability of Type II error.
  • p-value: evidence against H0.

5. Confidence Interval

A confidence interval estimates plausible parameter values around a sample estimate.

CI for mean (sigma known) = x-bar plus/minus Z * (sigma/sqrt(n))

Higher confidence or lower margin of error requires larger sample size.

6. Sample Size Determination

Sample size depends on confidence level, variability, and acceptable error.

n = (Z^2 * sigma^2) / E^2

Always round up to ensure required precision is satisfied.

7. Tests for Proportion

Used to compare sample proportion against a hypothesized population value.

Z = (p-hat - p0) / sqrt( p0(1-p0) / n )

8. Tests for Means

Includes one-sample mean tests and difference between two means, with hypothesis decision by critical value or p-value.

Reject H0 if p-value less than or equal to alpha

9. p-value Interpretation

p-value reflects the probability of obtaining observed evidence or stronger evidence assuming H0 is true. Smaller p-values imply stronger contradiction of H0.

Learning Outcomes Choose suitable sampling designs, construct confidence intervals, formulate and test hypotheses for means and proportions, and justify decisions using significance and p-value logic.
