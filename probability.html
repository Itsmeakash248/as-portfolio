Probability

Probability quantifies uncertainty by assigning a value between 0 and 1 to events in a random experiment. It enables evidence-based decisions under incomplete certainty.

Random experiment Sample space Events Conditional probability
Rules and formulas Solve with Python

Three interpretations

  • Classical: based on equally likely outcomes.
  • Empirical: based on observed frequencies.
  • Subjective: based on informed belief.

Core Rules

The following identities are the backbone of event probability calculations.

Addition Rule

Used to compute probability of at least one of two events occurring.

P(A union B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A intersection B)

For mutually exclusive events, the intersection term is zero.

P(A union B) = P(A) + P(B)

Multiplication Rule

Used to compute probability of two events occurring jointly.

Independent: P(A intersection B) = P(A) * P(B)
Dependent: P(A intersection B) = P(A) * P(B given A)

This distinction is crucial when sampling without replacement.

Conditional Probability

Conditional probability updates event likelihood after partial information is known.

P(A given B) = P(A intersection B) / P(B), where P(B) > 0

It formalizes information-driven decision making in uncertain systems.

Worked Example: Deck of Cards

Find the probability that a drawn card is either a king or a heart.

P(King) = 4/52, P(Heart) = 13/52, P(King and Heart) = 1/52
P(King or Heart) = 4/52 + 13/52 - 1/52 = 16/52 = 4/13

Worked Example: Coin and Die

Find probability of getting Head and an even number when tossing a coin and rolling a die.

P(Head) = 1/2, P(Even) = 3/6 = 1/2
P(Head and Even) = 1/2 * 1/2 = 1/4

Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' theorem reverses conditioning and helps infer causes from observed outcomes.

P(A given B) = [P(B given A) * P(A)] / P(B)

Applications: quality control, medical diagnosis, spam detection, and decision support under uncertainty.

Learning Outcomes Define random experiments and events, apply addition and multiplication rules, interpret conditional probability, and solve reverse-probability problems using Bayes' theorem.
